COURSE: CS109
TITLE: Introduction to Probability for Computer Scientists

LEVEL: undergraduate

DESCRIPTION:
Topics include: counting and combinatorics, random variables, conditional probability, independence, distributions (including Bernoulli, binomial, geometric, Poisson, uniform, exponential, normal, and joint distributions), expectation, variance, covariance, point estimation, maximum likelihood estimation, and limit theorems (law of large numbers, central limit theorem). Applications to computer science including hashing, load balancing, and Bayesian inference.
