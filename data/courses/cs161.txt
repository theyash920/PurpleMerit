TYPE: course

COURSE: CS161
TITLE: Design and Analysis of Algorithms

LEVEL: undergraduate

DESCRIPTION:
Worst and average case analysis. Recurrences and asymptotics. Efficient algorithms for sorting, searching, and selection. Data structures: binary search trees, heaps, hash tables, balanced trees. Algorithm design paradigms: divide and conquer, dynamic programming, greedy algorithms. Graph algorithms: shortest paths, minimum spanning trees, network flow. Intractability and NP-completeness. This is a core requirement for all CS degree programs at Stanford.

PREREQUISITES:
CS109 AND CS103
